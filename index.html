About

Club Swimming is a co-ed team with about 200 active members. We are part of College Club Swimming (CCS)
which is the collective organization that unifies college club swim programs across the country.

We practice five days per week for an hour and a half and compete in meets in both the fall and spring semesters.
There are three meets per semester, two of those being "day" meets where we travel to schools within Virginia and one
overnight/weekend meet.

We attend CCS Nationals in the spring semester every year.


How to Join

We do not hold tryouts nor do we make cuts.

Our team functions as a year-round team, so you can technically join throughout the year. The ideal time to
join is at the beginning of the semester. We hold a fall information session, where you can learn all about
the team. Beside the info session, you can come to practice and join the team any day during the week.

JMU Club Swim Email List

This is the best way to join / receive updates on the team.

In order to subscribe to the JMU Club Swim email list, please send an email to jmuclubswimming@gmail.com
Please make the subject "Email List" and provide your full name, phone number, and email address.

If you are interested in joining please state your interest in your email and one of our exec members will
respond to you shortly with more information.

In order to unsubscribe from the email list, please send an email with the subject "Unsubscribe" and list
your name in the body.

Dues

Dues are $85 per semester or $150 for the full year
This covers everything from weekend social events to travel expenses for meets and Nationals.

FAQS

Where and How Often Are Practices?

All practices are held in the University Recreation (UREC) Pool. We offer practices Monday through Friday
starting at 4 p.m. Our schedule varies per date and semester, so check the calendar tab to see the up to
date practice schedule.

How Many Meets Per Semester?

We attend multiple meets through out the season. We usually attend 2 to 3 in the fall and 3 to 4 in the
spring. We also host our own home meet in the Spring and host an Invitational in the Fall. We end our season
at the East Coast Collegiate Swim & Dive Club National Championship hosted by Georgia Tech in the Spring

How Does the Point System Work?

We function off a point system to make sure the most active members get the most benefits. We give points
for every practice attended, community service event, fundraiser, and other club sports' events attendance.
This is most often used when attending a meet with a cap on how many swimmers we can bring.
